Finals Week

My foot tapped on the hard tiled floor faster than humanly possible. It feels as if time is speeding up as I stand here in line. The cafeteria is so noisy. Yet, I can’t hear anyone. My mind instead is overwhelmed with math equations I need to remember. Next period I have a math final to take and that's all I have been able to think about. So much is riding on this one moment.

“Hey you’re up!” A voice came behind me.

Quickly I snapped out of the calculus nightmare. I glanced at the food options which sat on foam trays. Without much thought I chose a chicken sandwich, grabbed some ketchup and mayonnaise and called it good. I picked up the tray and started walking towards the commons area.

Before I knew it I was at the table my friends and I always sat at and I took a seat.

My friends all sat at the circular table in silence staring at notebooks that had been worn out through the current school year.

“How are you guys feeling about your tests?” I asked.

Jake looked up from his notebook and responded, “Pretty good. Luckily my teachers gave us all study guides of what is going to be on the tests. So in theory I should do fine.”

Myles pitched in, “Yeah my history teacher gave us a packet that has 50 questions to answer. He said 20 of those questions will be our final. You already know I have that packet memorized now!”

Laughing, I said, “Funny how much time our teachers spend making sure we do well on our tests(Engleman).”

“Hey I am all for it! Helps me get those good grades. Need to keep the grades up high so I can get some good scholarships!” Myles said.

We all nodded in agreement. Totally changing the subject we talked about everything else that was going on in our lives for the remainder of lunch.

“DING! DING! DING!” Rang the bell.

My heart dropped. Even though I was prepared for this test there was a lot on the line. I got up and threw away the foam tray. Slowly I made my way to B-hall. After making my way through the sea of students I took one last breath before entering class. I just needed to trust that I got this. Studying all day yesterday will certainly pay off. And with that thought I walked into class ready to take on what was in front of me.
