Editors Note

Hello! Welcome to my website all about education reform! This site is intended to be both informative and encourage you as the reader to do your own research on the topic. All of the writings that you will find on this site were researched, written and revised as I (Brayden Lemke) was taking English 2010 at Salt Lake Community College. You will find three different articles on this site. The first takes the mode of a flash memoir. This memoir is a memory that sets the tone of how I personally feel about education reform. Many ideas will be brought up that are typically heard from students in high school or college.

The second article you will find is a research paper about the history of education reform that is strictly informative. Reading over this writing will give you general knowledge of relevant actions that have been taken in the past 50 years to reform the education system. At the very end of the article I give you some questions to consider to help you form your own opinion on whether or not education reform is necessary and/or have past actions been positive or negative for the overall system.

Lastly, in the third article I give you my proposed plan to improve education as a whole. I go over some of the research that I have conducted on this topic and the conclusion that I have come to at this time. I then give 3 principles that I believe, if implemented properly, will have a huge impact on education as we know it. The goal of this article is to convince those who have the power to make large changes to implement reform that includes these principles as the focus.

After reading each of these articles I would hope that it sparks some familiarity as when you have gone to school and felt the same feelings that I have. The overall idea is that school should feel like a place to learn and to grow. To gain knowledge to better the world we live in. Instead it feels like a bunch of tests that need to be taken. Once the test has been taken the content learned is then forgotten. Overall education feels like a roadblock that must be overcome to be considered for well paying jobs.

I would love it if action was taken to shift the focus in the classroom away from tests and busy work. Instead the goal is to have classrooms that teach applicable content which students understand are important concepts to learn. Without students understanding the end goal of learning in any given subject there is no motivation to retain what is taught. Overall, I really hope you enjoy exploring this topic with me! I spent a lot of time writing and researching. If this topic interests you, be sure to do your own research and share your thoughts with your friends!

Many years ago school was once a hunting lesson. To learn how to survive. Over many years we have become so advanced that hunting is not the main lesson to be taught. Instead we teach each other how to build tall structures, how to manipulate complicated data, how we can learn from the great lessons of the past, and so much more!
